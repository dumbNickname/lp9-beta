## What forks must do

If you fork this project — which the AGPL fully permits — you **must
rename** your fork before redistributing or running it as a
network-accessible service. Specifically:

- Replace every occurrence of `APP_NAME` (and any later final project
  name) with a name of your own choosing.
- Do not use the project's logo, mirror-symmetric mark, or any
  confusingly similar variant.
- Do not represent your fork as the original project, its successor,
  or as endorsed by the original project's owner.

This is a standard pattern. Mastodon, Bitwarden, Nextcloud, and many
other AGPL projects carry equivalent trademark notices for the same
reason: AGPL alone does not address brand identity, and forks that
keep the original name create real user confusion.

## What you may do without permission

- Refer to this project by name in **descriptive** writing, reviews,

- Use unmodified screenshots in such contexts.
- Display the logo in a clearly attributive way (e.g. "this fork is
  derived from `APP_NAME`") so long as you are not claiming to be
  the original.

If you are unsure whether your use is permitted, open an issue and
ask before publishing.

## Registration status

The trademark is currently unregistered. Formal registration with
EUIPO and USPTO (Nice classes 9 and 42) will follow once the final
project name is selected. Until then, the protection asserted by this
notice rests on common-law trademark rights established by use in
commerce.
